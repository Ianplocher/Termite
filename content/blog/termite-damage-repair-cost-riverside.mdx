## What Drives Repair Costs Up

Several factors determine whether your repair bill will be on the lower or higher end:

### 1. Duration of the Infestation

Termites that have been active for months typically cause surface-level damage that is relatively affordable to fix. But termites that have been feeding for **years** can compromise structural members like floor joists, wall studs, and roof rafters — leading to repairs that cost five figures or more.

### 2. Location of the Damage

Damage to easily accessible areas like baseboards and window trim is straightforward to repair. Damage to structural elements buried inside walls, under floors, or in the attic requires more labor and often involves opening up walls, which adds drywall and painting costs to the total.

### 3. Type of Termite

**Drywood termites** tend to damage isolated areas of wood — often attic framing, window frames, or furniture. Repairs are usually more contained.

**Subterranean termites** can cause more widespread damage because their colonies are much larger (sometimes millions of individuals) and they attack wood from below, often damaging subfloors, floor joists, and sill plates before homeowners notice.

## Why Insurance Usually Does Not Cover Termite Damage

Most homeowners insurance policies in California **specifically exclude termite damage** from coverage. Insurance companies categorize termite damage as a "maintenance issue" rather than sudden accidental damage, which means the full cost of repairs falls on the homeowner.

This is one of the strongest arguments for regular termite inspections. A free inspection that catches termites early can save you thousands compared to discovering extensive damage years later.

## The Cost of Doing Nothing

The most expensive option is ignoring the problem. Termites do not stop eating. A colony that causes $2,000 in damage this year could cause $10,000 in damage two years from now. In extreme cases, untreated termite damage can render parts of a home structurally unsafe.

Riverside's warm climate means termites are active **year-round**, unlike colder regions where winter slows them down. There is no off-season for termite damage in the Inland Empire.

## How to Minimize Repair Costs

The most effective way to keep termite repair costs low is simple:

1. **Get annual termite inspections** to catch activity early
2. **Address moisture issues** around your foundation immediately
3. **Remove wood-to-soil contact** wherever possible
4. **Fix leaky plumbing** and ensure proper drainage
5. **Act quickly** if you notice any signs of termite activity

A free inspection today could save you thousands in repair costs down the road.
