Buying a home is the biggest financial decision most people will ever make — and in Riverside, CA, a termite inspection should be at the top of your due diligence checklist. Termite damage is one of the most common issues found during the home buying process in the Inland Empire, and catching it before you close can save you tens of thousands of dollars.

## Why Lenders Require Termite Inspections

Most mortgage lenders in California — especially those offering FHA, VA, and USDA loans — require a **Wood Destroying Organism (WDO) report** before they will approve financing. This report, sometimes called a "Section 1" report, documents any active termite infestations or visible damage to the property.

Even if your lender does not explicitly require one, getting a termite inspection before buying a Riverside home is one of the smartest moves you can make as a buyer.

## What Happens During a Pre-Purchase Inspection

A licensed inspector will examine the entire property, including:

- **Foundation and exterior walls** for mud tubes and entry points
- **Attic and roof framing** for drywood termite activity
- **Crawlspace and subfloor** for moisture damage and subterranean termites
- **Window frames, door frames, and baseboards** for signs of hidden damage
- **Garage and any detached structures** like sheds or pergolas

The inspection typically takes 30 to 45 minutes for an average-sized Riverside home.

## Section 1 vs Section 2 Findings

Your termite inspection report will categorize findings into two sections:

**Section 1** findings are active infestations or infections, including:
- Live termite activity
- Visible termite damage
- Active fungus or dry rot

**Section 2** findings are conditions likely to lead to future infestation:
- Excessive moisture in crawlspaces
- Wood-to-soil contact at the foundation
- Faulty grading that directs water toward the structure

Lenders typically require Section 1 items to be treated before the loan closes. Section 2 items are recommendations but are usually not mandatory for closing.

## Who Pays for the Termite Inspection?

In Riverside County, it is negotiable between the buyer and seller. However, the most common arrangements are:

- **Buyer pays** for the inspection itself
- **Seller pays** for any required Section 1 treatment or repairs
- **Both parties negotiate** if significant damage is found

## Riverside Neighborhoods to Watch

Some Riverside neighborhoods have higher rates of termite activity due to older construction, mature trees, and established landscaping. Areas like **Wood Streets**, **Arlington Heights**, **Mission Grove**, and **Canyon Crest** are particularly worth inspecting carefully due to the age of homes and the abundance of wood-framed construction.

Newer developments in areas like **Orangecrest** and **La Sierra South** are not immune either — subterranean termites can infest homes of any age.

## What to Do If Termites Are Found

Finding termites during a home inspection does not mean you should walk away from the deal. Here is a smart approach:

1. **Review the inspection report** carefully with your real estate agent
2. **Get treatment estimates** from a licensed pest control company
3. **Negotiate with the seller** to cover treatment costs or reduce the sale price
4. **Request a re-inspection** after treatment to confirm the issue is resolved
5. **Factor ongoing prevention** into your homeownership budget

## Protect Your Investment Before You Close

A termite inspection is a small step that can prevent a massive financial headache. Whether you are buying your first home in Riverside or your fifth, scheduling an inspection before closing is always worth it.
